The Conart Gallery


"The purpose of art is washing the dust of daily life off our souls."

 
French Artist Pablo Picasso
 

ConArt Gallery is one of the leading contemporary galleries in Ireland. The Gallery was founded on the 20th of May 1950 by a diverse collective of graduates from the Limerick School of Art and design and has grown from strength to strength since opening. The Gallery houses an impressive permanent collection of Irish artist masterpieces dating from the 18th to 20th century. The artwork comes in a range of mediums and media. The Gallery also hosts contemporary exhibitions with Irish and international artists throughout the calendar year. Our goal is to promote culture and creativity in the city.

 

In visiting the Gallery, you are encouraged to explore the exhibitions and ask questions to the team if a piece of art takes your interest. Tours can be arranged if requested via email. The ConArtist Gallery encourages enquiry and supports creativity. The Gallery is open Monday to Saturday from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM. However, if it’s the opening of an exhibition or an auction the gallery is open until 11:00 PM.

 

Membership at the ConArt Gallery offers V.I.P access to new exhibitions, promotions, opening shows, auctions, and the opportunity to attend limited seated panel talks with the artists themselves. The Membership ranges from a three-month bronze member plan to a six-month silver member plan, to a yearlong gold member plan.

 

The Gallery has a weekly newsletter with upcoming events and news, you can sign up for it on the ConArtist online website.

 

The ConArt website holds a detailed account of photographs and descriptions of artwork for sale in the gallery. If you wish to purchase a piece of art, click the “buy” button, to order online. The objective of the online e-commerce gallery is to make purchasing artwork as easy as possible for collectors.

 

The Gallery is in the epicenter of Corks bustling city, housed in the Redemptorist building on the grounds of Fr. Michael’s Park. A cafe is located on site which offers a range of tasty beverages and treats. The Gallery is fully accessible to the public, a lift and disabled toilet facilities are found inside.
